Does Dungeons &amp; Dragons: Honor Among Thieves have a post-credits scene?

Publish date: 2024-08-17

The Dungeons & Dragons franchise is about to become even bigger with the release of Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves. The film, which was written and directed by Jonathan Goldstein and John Francis Daley, sees a group of misfits come together to battle a rising evil. Honor Among Thieves is set to release later this month, on March 31. You can read our review of the film here.

The main classes of Dungeons & Dragons are represented in the film, with Chris Pine playing a bard named Edgin, Michelle Rodriguez as a barbarian named Holga, Justice Smith playing a sorcerer named Simon, Sophia Lillis as a tiefling druid named Doric, Regé-Jean Page playing a paladin named Xenk, and Hugh Grant as a rogue called Forge.

Post-credits scenes have become a staple of blockbuster popcorn movies thanks to Marvel’s popularization of them, but does Honor Among Thieves have one?

Is there anything after the credits of Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves?

You will not have to wait until the end of the credits of Honor Among Thieves, but there is a post-credits scene. After the main characters get their title credits, a short, final scene awaits those still seated.

The sequence isn’t pivotal to the film’s overarching plot, and largely leans into the trope of providing a humorous bonus to anyone waiting around to see if their patience will be rewarded. In fact, one of the very last scenes in the movie acts as a post-credits scene would so if you’re in a rush to beat everyone else out of the parking lot, a quick Google search back home might be more worthwhile.

You can see the scenes yourself when Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves flies into theaters on March 31, 2023.
