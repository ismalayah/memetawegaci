New York University Graduate Jill Sprecher: Early Life 

Jill Sprecher was born in 1959s to father Pete Sprecher and mother Phyllis Sprecher. She grew up in Madison, Wisconsin, the United States of America, with her two siblings, sister Karen Sprecher and brother Jeffrey C. Sprecher. 

Her father, Pete Sprecher, was an insurance agent. The family moved from time to time, depending on where the company posted Pete. 

Jill Sprecher is around 61 years old. She holds an American nationality and belongs to a white ethnic background. 

Regarding her education, Jill Sprecher went to James Madison Memorial High School, Madison, Wisconsin. Later, she attended the University of Wisconsin and studied literature and philosophy. She relocated to New York to study cinema. She graduated from New York University with a Master’s degree in Cinema Studies. 

Jill Sprecher’s Career Highlights

After graduation, Jill Sprecher struggled in New York City. To support herself and afford rent, she took some temporary jobs in the film industry. In her struggle phase, Sprecher was mugged twice. After she was mugged second time in 2002, she required brain surgery. In the same year, she did the surgery. 

Eventually, Sprecher got to work in positions like production coordinator and production assistant. She then hired her sister Karen as an assistant production coordinator. It was revealed that two sisters used to write the American comedy-drama film Clockwatchers in their leisure time. 

Finally, Jill’s first directorial debut movie Clockwatchers was premiered in 1997. The film starred Lisa Kudrow and won several international awards. In 2001, her second drama, Thirteen Conservations About One Thing, was released. 

Did You Know? Jill Sprecher served as the judge for CableACE Awards for five years. 

As of now, American director cum writer serves as freelance writers-for-hire based in Los Angeles, Southern California.
